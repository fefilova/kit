ABOUT PUGS
Pugs miraculously combine the charm of a small-sized dog and the courage of a true loyal dog. They are smart and strive for independence,
so sometimes it is difficult to train. However, these dogs love to learn something new, the main thing is not to show excessive persistence and rigidity.
But if you have a pug, be sure - now you will not be bored! This little clown will amuse the whole family. He knows how to snore very funny, sneeze, sniff
and even grunt! Particular attention should be paid to the diet of the dog - pugs tend to be overweight.
BREED HISTORY
The pug is an ancient Chinese breed. Early Chinese manuscripts mention "square, short dogs with a short snout." In China
there were dogs "Ha Pa" and "Lo Jie". These two varieties were very similar and differed only in the length of the coat. "Ha Pa" were
long-haired. Probably the ancestors of pugs were "Luo Jie", they looked like Pekingese, but had short hair. Pugs were the dogs of the nobility
and lived in wealthy homes. In those days, pugs did not yet have such deep wrinkles, but a clear pattern of folds on the forehead appeared
and was similar to hieroglyphs. That is why the wrinkles on the pug's forehead were called the imperial sign. The pug came to France with the
Turkish fleet in 1553. Later this breed became loved in the Netherlands, where its color was likened to the colors of the ruling house of Orange.
When William III of Orange became king of England, he and his wife Maria II brought pugs from the Netherlands in 1689. This breed has been very
popular for about two centuries. But gradually the pugs became smaller. In 1864, for the English Queen Victoria, who wished to have this breed,
they hardly found one dog. After 20 years, the first pug lovers club was created, and thanks to him, the breed began to improve and acquire the
tandards that are still appreciated in these dogs. Napoleon's first wife, Empress Josephine, had a beloved pug named Fortuna.
